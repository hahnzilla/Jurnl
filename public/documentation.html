    6. Introduction

      Donut is a one-stop shop for writers of all skill levels to improve and track their writing habits over time. With configurable word count and words/minute goals, hash tagging and intuitive search tools, Donut has everything you'll need to effectively chart and improve your journaling skills in a clear, consolidated approach.

      Donut works by providing its users with a private online repository of journal entries. As users populate their repositories, the system logs information about the users' habits on each entry including details like number of distractions, number of spelling errors and average words per minute. The Donut editor may be configured to display this information in real time, providing immediate feedback to writers more aggressively tracking their skills.

      In addition to these performance tracking features, Donut is also a convenient cloud-based journaling solution for the casual writer. The editor supports all common phrase-level and block-level formatting that a journaler might need including lists, blockquotes, monospaced and verbatim; color, size and emphasized text. With Donut, your work is available anywhere, anytime, exportable in text and HTML formats.

      Whether you are actively pursuing improved writing skills, curious about your abilities, or just want a convenient, available-everywhere writing collection, take a bite out of Donut, and experience the sweet solution.

    Creating and Editing an Entry

      To get started, click on the new entry button on your homepage. This will open a text editor that allows you to create and edit an entry.

      The following editing features are available to use:

    • Font Tools
    • Layout Tools
    • Formatting Tools

      If you have finished the entry, press the floppy disk icon to save the entry.

    Tagging Entries

    Tag any word within an entry by putting a # infront of the word that you wish to tag. Hashtagging a word within a post will make that word searchable.

    Searching Entries

    You can search your entries for the following:

    • Hash-tagged words
    • Content of an entry
    • Date an entry was posted

    Statistical Analysis

      Distractions

      Distractions are counted for every second there is not a keystroke detected after 3 minutes. To decrease your distractions while typing, close out of all programs that you would be likely to switch to during your journal entry. It might also be helpful to brainstorm before beginning your entry so you don't run out of ideas to type about.
